## 8. SEO & Domain Strategy

### Domain Naming: No "NZ" in Names, .co.nz TLD

**No "NZ" in the domain name itself** (e.g., no `aifortradesnz` or `nzaibusiness`). The literal letters "NZ" in a domain name look amateurish and waste keyword space. The .co.nz TLD already tells Google and users this is a New Zealand business.

**Why .co.nz TLD (not .com):**
- **We're selling to NZ businesses.** A tradie in Auckland seeing `aifortrades.co.nz` thinks "local, Kiwi." Seeing `aifortrades.com` thinks "could be anyone, anywhere."
- **.co.nz signals trust and locality** — both to Google's local algorithm and to NZ business owners who prefer buying from local providers.
- **.co.nz is cheaper** — ~$25-40 NZD/yr vs $15-20 USD (~$25-33 NZD) for .com. Minor difference, but no reason to pay more for something we don't need yet.
- **International expansion is a future problem.** If we enter AU in Phase 2 (Month 18+), we buy the .com versions then. That's a $15/domain problem in the future, not a strategic decision today.
- **Focus on what matters now:** Auckland tradies picking up the phone, not AU optionality we're 18+ months away from needing.

## 13. Revenue Model & Unit Economics (Detailed Cost Breakdown)

### Cost Absorption Policy

**Decision: Absorb all tool and model costs into the retainer margin. Do NOT pass through to clients.**

**Reasoning:**
1. **Cleaner pricing** — Clients pay a fixed monthly fee. No invoice surprises. No confusion.
2. **Justifies the retainer** — The monthly fee isn't just "maintenance." It covers infrastructure, continuous model improvements, and usage. That's what the retainer buys.
3. **Control over model selection** — When we absorb costs, we can switch models (e.g., GPT-4o → Claude Haiku) without notifying clients. This is our primary margin-preservation lever.
4. **Competitive positioning** — Competitors who pass through costs feel transactional. We feel like partners.
5. **Simplicity at scale** — As client count grows, variable usage billing would create accounting complexity. Fixed costs per tier = clean books.

**Risk mitigation:** Include a usage cap clause in contracts. If a client's Vapi usage exceeds 500 min/month (Tier 1) or 700 min/month (Tier 2), a usage review is triggered. In practice, this will rarely be needed but protects against edge cases.

---

### Model Selection Strategy (Margin Preservation)

The biggest operational margin lever is **which LLM model we use for each task**. This is internal decision-making, invisible to clients.

## 16. Risks & Mitigations

### Risk 1: Co-Founder Falls Through (HIGH probability concern, HIGH impact)

**This is the biggest underweighted risk in V3.** The entire dual-vertical strategy, delivery capacity, and 50-client target assumes a capable co-founder is in place from Day 1.

**Mitigation:**
- Don't launch dual verticals until co-founder is contracted (SHA signed)
- If co-founder falls through before launch: immediately revert to single-vertical solo model (Trades — highest ROI channel for solo founder)
- Ace's autonomous delivery capability partially compensates — Anthony + Ace can handle 15–20 clients solo
- Set a co-founder recruitment deadline: if no co-founder by Day 30, proceed solo in Trades with modified targets (25–30 clients Year 1)

### Risk 2: Dual Vertical Spreading-Too-Thin (MEDIUM probability, HIGH impact)

Addressed honestly in Section 12. The Day 45 Traction Check is the primary control.

**Mitigation:**
- Day 45 Traction Check is mandatory — not optional
- If neither vertical has 5 clients: consolidate immediately
- Ace's build speed helps — demo builds for both verticals happen simultaneously in Week 1

### Risk 3: AI Tools Change Fast (HIGH probability, MEDIUM impact)

**Mitigation:** Anthony's tech radar role. Multi-tool stack. OpenClaw as orchestration layer with 2–3 substitutes for each function. Ace can reconfigure the stack when models improve.

### Risk 4: Platform Vendors Build Native AI (HIGH probability, MEDIUM-HIGH impact)

**Mitigation:** We'll be entrenched in client operations before this happens. Switching costs (technical + relational) are painful. As basic AI commoditises, we move upmarket to complex OpenClaw PA configurations.

### Risk 5: Vapi Voice Costs Exceed Projections (MEDIUM probability, MEDIUM impact)

**Mitigation:** Usage monitoring from Day 1. Include usage cap clause in contracts (>500 min/month triggers review). Model selection inside Vapi (cheaper STT/TTS providers where quality permits). Switch to Bland.ai if Vapi pricing increases significantly.

### Risk 6: Delivery Quality Slips at Speed (HIGH risk if not managed)

**Mitigation:** SOP library built in Week 1. First 5 clients per vertical get white-glove service — no rushing. Scale velocity only after Case Study #1 is documented. The 20-client capacity cap keeps quality intact before first hire.

### Risk 7: Client Churn if Results Don't Show (LOW if executed well, HIGH impact)

**Mitigation:** Only take clients where ROI is measurable. Track metrics from Day 1. First 5 clients: go above and beyond. Their testimonials fund everything else.

### Risk 8: Competition Intensifies (MEDIUM, 6–12 months)

**Mitigation:** Build vertical moat NOW. Get 10 trade clients and 5 RE clients in the first 90 days with case studies attached. That depth is not catchable quickly. The SEO microsite strategy creates inbound organic moat by Month 6+.

### Risk 9: NZ Economy Softness (MEDIUM — ongoing)

**Mitigation:** AI that saves $10,000–$15,000/month is recession-proof. Cost-saving and efficiency tools sell better in a soft economy. Our ROI pitch is counter-cyclical.

### Risk 10: LLM Model Pricing Increases (LOW-MEDIUM)

**Mitigation:** Absorbing model costs creates this exposure. We mitigate via: (a) model selection discipline (cheaper models for routine tasks), (b) contract usage caps, (c) annual pricing reviews, (d) the ability to switch models without client notification. If Anthropic or OpenAI significantly raises prices, we route more traffic through the cheaper provider.
