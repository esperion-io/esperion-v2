## PART 1: VIDEO LEARNINGS — What the Video Actually Teaches

### The Core Framework

The video is a 5-part series covering the full lifecycle of Facebook ads for service businesses. The author runs ads for local service businesses (HVAC, tree trimming, cleaning, dog grooming) and teaches from direct spend experience — not theory.

**The hierarchy that governs everything:**
> Goal > Audience > Creative (in that order, always)

If your campaign objective is wrong, the best creative in the world won't save you. If your audience is wrong, the best copy won't save you. Most people start with creative and work backwards — that's why they fail.

### The 5 Parts Summarised

**Part 1 — Foundations:**
- Every ad needs alignment: clear problem + relevant offer + right person at the right time
- 3-tier structure: Campaign (the why) → Ad Set (the who/when/where) → Ad (the what)
- Pick the right campaign objective or Facebook literally optimises for the wrong people
- Never boost a post — always use Meta Ads Manager
- Answer 5 questions before you ever click publish

**Part 2 — Build Your First Campaign:**
- Step-by-step Ads Manager walkthrough
- Start with Messages objective for service businesses
- $10-$20/day starting budget
- Manual placements: Facebook Feed, Instagram Feed, Instagram Stories only
- 72-hour learning phase — don't touch anything

**Part 3 — Writing Ads That Convert:**
- 4-part copy framework: Hook → Problem → Offer → CTA
- Write at 5th-7th grade reading level
- Real photos beat stock images every time
- Headlines: problem-first, results-focused, or offer-forward
- Sound like a person, not a corporation

**Part 4 — Testing & Optimisation:**
- 72-hour rule: don't touch ads for 72 hours
- 5 metrics that matter: CTR (>1%), CPC (<$2), CPM ($10-25), Link Clicks vs Page Views, ROAS
- AB test ONE variable at a time, in order: Creative → Caption → Headline → Audience → Placement → Objective
- Retargeting warm traffic at $3-5/day
- Refresh creatives every 2-3 weeks

**Part 5 — Scaling & Automation:**
- Scale when: CTR >1%, cost per lead is profitable, results consistent 5-7 days, actual customers not just engagement
- Scale slowly: 20-30% budget increase every 3 days, OR duplicate ad set with higher budget
- 3-part lead handling: Auto-reply, CRM/tracker, template responses
- Funnel: Cold ad (top) → Retargeting (warm) → Nurture (follow-up)

**Bonus — Shortcuts:**
1. Stalk competitors in Meta Ad Library
2. Run PPE (engagement) campaigns to build social proof, then reuse post ID in cold campaigns
3. Duplicate winners fast, change one thing
4. Use customer testimonial videos (15 sec, vertical, authentic)
5. Skip expensive testing platforms — CTR + CPM + comments tell you enough
6. Test ad hooks as organic tweets/posts first
7. "Sell it before you build it" — run ads to validate demand

## PART 2: APPLIED TO AI TOOLS NZ — Our Specific Inputs

### The 5 Pre-Launch Questions (Answered for AI Tools NZ)

**1. Who is this ad for?**
NZ business owners (1-50 employees) in Auckland who are AI-curious but haven't made AI work. Primary: trades/home services owners and real estate agency principals. Secondary: property managers, medical/dental practice owners, recruitment firms.

**2. What problem do they have right now?**
They've tried ChatGPT. Maybe played with a chatbot. But they're still doing everything manually — answering every call themselves, chasing leads by hand, doing admin that could be automated. They're losing $5K-$15K/month in missed opportunities because they can't respond fast enough or handle the volume.

**3. What solution am I offering?**
An AI system built specifically for how their business runs — answers calls they miss, responds to leads in seconds (not hours), automates the admin grind so they can focus on billable work. Setup in 5-7 days, not 4-8 weeks. Ongoing support and improvement included.

**4. What proof do I have?**
- 82% of NZ businesses experimenting with AI, only 12% made it work — we're the 12%
- Live demo on demo.aitools.co.nz
- Government co-funding available (RBP Network up to $15K)
- The "tradie missing 6 calls a day = $15K/month in lost jobs" stat
- Real estate: respond in 60 seconds vs 2 hours = 70% more listing conversions

**5. What is the one action I want them to take?**
Message us to book a free 15-minute demo call (Calendly).

### Campaign Objective Selection

**Messages** — this is the right choice for us. Here's why:
- We're a service business selling high-ticket B2B ($2K-$14K setup + $500-$3,500/month)
- We need conversations, not clicks
- Messages gives us instant feedback on offer resonance
- We can qualify leads directly in the conversation before booking a call
- The video specifically says: "If you're a local business, 90% of the time start with messages"

### Ad Set Configuration — AI Tools NZ Specific

| Field | Our Selection | Reasoning |
|-------|--------------|-----------|
| **Conversion location** | Messaging apps | Messages objective → messenger/Instagram DMs |
| **Daily budget** | $20/day (Week 1-2), scale to $50 if metrics hit | $20 gets us learning data fast without burning cash. We're selling high-ticket so even 1 client/month ROIs massively |
| **Location** | Auckland + 15km radius | 35% of NZ trade businesses are here. Our core market. Can expand to NZ-wide later |
| **Age** | 30-65+ | Business owners skew older. Under 30s rarely own trade businesses or RE agencies |
| **Gender** | All | No reason to filter — both men and women own businesses in our verticals |
| **Detailed targeting** | Start broad. Optional: "Small business owner" or "Business page admin" | Facebook knows who messages businesses. Let the algorithm find them. Don't narrow with "AI" interests — that's tech enthusiasts, not buyers |
| **Placements** | Manual: Facebook Feed, Instagram Feed, Instagram Stories | Per the video — avoid Messenger inbox, Audience Network, Reels. These drive cheap clicks but not conversions. Marketplace could work but test later |

### The Offer (Tightened for AI Tools NZ)

**Bad offer:** "We offer AI implementation services for NZ businesses. Contact us to learn more."
→ Too vague, no timeline, no hook, no specific problem solved. Garbage.

**Good offer:** "Missing calls? Losing leads? We'll build you an AI system that answers every enquiry in under 60 seconds — set up in 5 days, not 5 weeks. Book a free 15-min demo and see it work live."
→ Specific problem, specific timeline, specific proof mechanism, clear CTA.

## PART 3: STEP-BY-STEP EXECUTION GUIDE

### Phase 0: Pre-Launch Setup (Before Spending $1)

**Step 1: Meta Business Suite Setup**
- Go to business.facebook.com
- Ensure AI Tools NZ has a Facebook Business Page and linked Instagram account
- Access Meta Ads Manager (not the "Boost" button)
- Set up Meta Pixel on aitools.co.nz (for retargeting later)
- Connect Calendly booking link

**Step 2: Competitive Intelligence**
- Go to Meta Ad Library (facebook.com/ads/library)
- Search for: NZ AI agencies, NZ digital agencies, ChatGPT services NZ, automation consultants
- Document: What creatives are they using? Long or short copy? What hooks? How long have their ads been running? (Long-running = working)
- Also search Australian competitors for more data points

**Step 3: Answer the 5 Questions** (done above)

**Step 4: Prepare Creative Assets** (see Part 5 below for full asset list)

### Phase 1: Launch — First Campaign (Days 1-3)

**Step 1: Create Campaign**
- Click green "+ Create" in Ads Manager
- Objective: Messages
- Name: `AI Tools NZ — Messages — Mar 2026`
- Special Ad Categories: Leave blank (we're not in real estate/employment/credit)
- AB Test: Skip for now
- Advantage Campaign Budget: OFF

**Step 2: Configure Ad Set**
- Conversion location: Messaging apps
- Daily budget: $20
- Start date: Today, no end date
- Audience: DO NOT use Advantage+ yet
  - Location: Auckland, New Zealand + 15km radius
  - Age: 30-65+
  - Gender: All
  - Detailed targeting: Broad (or add "Small business owner" if you want)
- Placements: Manual
  - ✅ Facebook Feed
  - ✅ Instagram Feed
  - ✅ Instagram Stories
  - ❌ Everything else

**Step 3: Create the Ad**
- Select correct Facebook Page + Instagram account
- Format: Single image (not carousel, not slideshow)
- Upload creative (see asset section)
- Primary text (caption): Use Ad Copy Version A (below)
- Headline: Problem-first headline
- CTA button: Send Message

**Step 4: Review & Publish**
- Double-check: Location, budget, targeting, placements, creative, copy
- Click Publish
- Facebook reviews (usually <24 hours)
- **DO NOT TOUCH FOR 72 HOURS** (learning phase)

### Phase 2: Monitor & Learn (Days 4-10)

**After 72 hours, check these 5 metrics:**

| Metric | Target | If Below Target |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| CTR (Click-through rate) | >1% | Hook or image is weak — test new creative |
| CPC (Cost per click/message) | <$5 (we're B2B high-ticket, so higher CPC is OK) | Creative or targeting issue |
| CPM (Cost per 1,000 impressions) | $10-$30 | If >$40, targeting too narrow — go broader |
| Messages received | 1+ per day at $20 spend | If zero after 3 days, offer or creative needs work |
| Message quality | Qualified business owners, not randoms | If unqualified, tighten copy to filter better |

**Troubleshooting:**
- Nobody clicking → Change image first, then strengthen hook
- Clicking but not messaging → CTA unclear, simplify copy, make offer stronger
- Messaging but not booking → Reply faster, use templates, qualify better in conversation
- Clicks too expensive → Broader targeting, try different image, shorter copy

### Phase 3: Test & Optimise (Days 10-21)

**AB Testing Order (one variable at a time):**

1. **Creative first** — Test Version A image vs Version B image (split budget evenly, run 3-5 days, keep winner)
2. **Caption next** — Test short copy vs long copy (same winning image)
3. **Headline** — Test problem-first vs results-focused
4. **Audience** — Test Auckland-only vs NZ-wide
5. **Placement** — Test adding Marketplace

**How to AB test:**
- Option A: Use Meta's built-in AB testing (Experiments tab)
- Option B: Duplicate the ad set, change ONE variable, split budget 50/50, run 3-5 days

### Phase 4: Retargeting (Day 14+)

Once you have a few hundred impressions/clicks:

**Create a retargeting ad:**
- Audience: Custom audience of people who interacted with your ads but didn't message
- Also: Instagram profile visitors, website visitors (if pixel is installed)
- Budget: $5/day
- Creative: More personal, more urgency
- Example copy: "Still thinking about how AI could work for your business? We've got 3 demo slots open this week. No pressure — just a 15-min call to see it live. Message us to grab one."

### Phase 5: Scale (Day 21+)

**Only scale when ALL of these are true:**
- CTR >1%
- Cost per message/lead is profitable (for us: even $50/lead is fine if 1 in 5 converts to a $8K+ Year 1 client)
- Results consistent for 5-7 days
- Getting actual business owner enquiries, not just curiosity clicks

**Scaling method:**
- Increase budget by 20-30% every 3 days
- OR duplicate winning ad set with 2-3x budget, let both run
- Monitor for 72 hours after each scale
- If metrics drop, scale back to last stable number

### ROI Math — Why This Works for Us

**Our numbers:**
- Average Year 1 client value: $8,000-$26,000 (Tier 1-2)
- Conservative: $12,000 average
- Close rate on qualified leads: assume 20% (1 in 5 demos → client)
- Cost per message/lead target: $5-$15

**At $20/day spend ($600/month):**
- Expected messages: 40-120/month (at $5-$15 per message)
- Qualified leads (assume 50% qualification rate): 20-60
- Demos booked (assume 50% of qualified): 10-30
- Clients closed (20% close rate): 2-6
- Revenue from those clients: $24,000-$72,000 Year 1 value
- **ROAS: 40x-120x**

Even at HALF those conversion rates, we're looking at $12K-$36K revenue on $600 ad spend. The unit economics are absurd because we're selling high-ticket B2B.

**When to increase spend:**
- Increase when: cost per qualified lead is stable and you have capacity to handle more demos
- Don't increase when: leads are unqualified, you can't respond fast enough, or cost per lead is rising
- Maximum recommended monthly spend in Phase 1-2: $1,500 (don't outpace delivery capacity)

### The Critical Rule on Budget

> "The difference between an ad that makes you $200 and an ad that makes you $20,000 is execution and repetition."

Don't throw $500 at it hoping for magic. Start at $20/day, learn what works, then scale deliberately. Every dollar should be teaching you something.
