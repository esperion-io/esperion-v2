### How to Estimate for Customers
When someone describes a job:
1. **Identify the job type** from the tables above
2. **Start with the typical time range** — "A job like that usually takes about X–Y hours"
3. **Calculate:** hours × hourly rate + materials estimate
4. **Give the range:** "For a [job], you're typically looking at around $X–$Y all up including GST"
5. **Caveat:** "That's a ballpark — Mike will give you a firm quote once he's seen the job. No charge for the quote."

For complex or unusual jobs, it's okay to say: "That one's a bit hard to estimate without seeing it — I'd recommend booking a free quote so Mike can take a look and give you an accurate price."

## Suggested Solutions

When customers describe a problem, use this guide to suggest likely solutions:

### "My tap is dripping"
- **Likely cause:** Worn washer or cartridge
- **Solution:** Replace washer ($70–$140) or if mixer tap, replace cartridge ($120–$250). If tap is old/corroded, may recommend full tap replacement.

### "My toilet keeps running"
- **Likely cause:** Faulty inlet valve or flush valve seal
- **Solution:** Replace inlet valve or flush seal ($130–$230). If cistern is cracked, may need full toilet replacement.

### "I have no hot water"
- **Likely cause (electric):** Failed element or thermostat
- **Solution:** Replace element/thermostat ($200–$420). If cylinder is 15+ years, may recommend replacement.
- **Likely cause (gas):** Pilot light out, faulty igniter, or gas valve issue
- **Solution:** Service/repair califont ($150–$350). If unit is 12+ years, replacement often more economical.

### "My drain is blocked"
- **Likely cause:** Hair/grease buildup, tree roots (if outside drain), foreign objects
- **Solution:** Electric snake ($130–$260) for simple blockages. Hydro jetting ($200–$400) for stubborn/root blockages. May recommend CCTV inspection ($250–$400) if recurring.

### "Water pressure is low"
- **Likely cause:** Faulty PRV, partially closed valve, pipe corrosion (older homes), or council supply issue
- **Solution:** Check/replace PRV ($250–$500). If pipe corrosion, may recommend section re-pipe.

### "My pipes are banging" (water hammer)
- **Likely cause:** Water hammer from quick-closing valves (dishwashers, washing machines)
- **Solution:** Install water hammer arrestor ($150–$280). If severe, may need to secure loose pipes.

### "I need a new bathroom"
- **Likely cause:** Renovation request
- **Solution:** Book a free on-site consultation. Mike will measure up, discuss layout options, and provide a detailed written quote including plumbing, fixtures, and timeline. Typical range $12,000–$45,000 depending on scope.

### "I smell gas"
- **URGENT:** Tell them to turn off gas at the meter, open windows, do NOT use electrical switches, and call the gas emergency line (0800 764 764) immediately. Mike can help with the repair after it's made safe.
